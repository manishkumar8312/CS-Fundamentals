# Code of Conduct

## 1. Our Commitment

We are committed to fostering an open, respectful, and inclusive environment for everyone who contributes to or engages with this repository. Our goal is to support learning, collaboration, and the sharing of knowledge in Computer Science fundamentals.

## 2. Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

* Communicate in a respectful and professional manner
* Provide constructive, relevant, and thoughtful feedback
* Contribute in a way that enhances the quality and clarity of the repository
* Respect diverse perspectives and experiences

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

* Harassment, discrimination, or offensive comments of any kind
* Personal attacks, insults, or abusive language
* Submission of irrelevant, low-quality, or spam content
* Plagiarism or failure to provide proper attribution

## 4. Contribution Standards

To maintain a high standard of quality:

* Contributions should be relevant to Computer Science fundamentals
* Content should be clear, concise, and well-structured
* Formatting and organization should remain consistent
* Proper credit must be given for any referenced material

## 5. Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct and may take appropriate action in response to violations, including:

* Editing or removing contributions
* Temporarily or permanently restricting contributor access

## 6. Reporting Issues

If you experience or observe behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by opening an issue or contacting the repository maintainer. All reports will be handled with discretion.

## 7. Acknowledgment

By participating in this repository, you agree to abide by this Code of Conduct and contribute to a positive and collaborative environment.
